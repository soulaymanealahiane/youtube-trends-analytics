Phase 1: Architectural Optimization

Theory

The raw dataset contained heavy unstructured text fields that were irrelevant to numerical analytics.

Action: We dropped kind, title, video_tags, thumbnail_url, and description.

Methodology

We used Stream Processing (Chunking) to read the file in blocks of 100,000 rows. This avoided loading the entire 5GB into RAM.

Code: Stream Processing

remove_columns.py
def drop_columns_stream(input_path, chunksize=100_000):
    TO_DROP = {"kind", "title", "video_tags", 
               "thumbnail_url", "description"}

    with open(input_path, "r") as fh:
        # Process in chunks to prevent MemoryError
        for chunk in pd.read_csv(fh, chunksize=chunksize):
            
            # Drop columns if they exist
            drop_here = [c for c in TO_DROP if c in chunk.columns]
            chunk = chunk.drop(columns=drop_here)
            
            # Write to new CSV
            chunk.to_csv(out_path, mode="a")

Phase 4: Feature Engineering

Standardization

Comparing raw counts is unfair (Big Channel vs. Small Channel). We engineered Rates to measure efficiency.

New Metrics

  • Engagement Rate: (Likes + Comments) / Views
  • Like Ratio: Likes / Views
  • Comment Ratio: Comments / Views

Code: Calculating Rates

stats_columns_addition.py (Part 1)
# 1. Avoid Division by Zero
df['view_count'] = df['view_count'].replace(0, 1)

# 2. Calculate Engagement Rate
df['engagement_rate'] = (
    (df['like_count'] + df['comment_count']) / 
    df['view_count']
).round(4)

# 3. Calculate Like Ratio
df['like_ratio'] = (
    df['like_count'] / df['view_count']
).round(4)

Phase 5: Outlier Handling

The Viral Anomaly

"Viral" videos are statistical outliers. Removing them destroys the analysis.

Strategy: We used the Interquartile Range (IQR) method to Flag them instead of deleting them.

The Flag

is_view_outlier (Boolean)

Allows Tableau users to toggle between "Normal Trends" and "Viral Anomalies".

Code: Outlier Flagging

stats_columns_addition.py (Part 2)
print("Identifying Outliers...")

Q1 = df['view_count'].quantile(0.25)
Q3 = df['view_count'].quantile(0.75)
IQR = Q3 - Q1

# Dynamic Upper Bound
upper = Q3 + 1.5 * IQR

# Create Flag Column
df['is_view_outlier'] = np.where(
    (df['view_count'] > upper), 
    True, 
    False
)

Section III

Business Insights &
Strategic Recommendations

Executive Summary of Findings

Following the rigorous data preparation and visualization in Tableau, our analysis of the Global YouTube Trends dataset reveals a critical narrative:

Reach does not equal resonance.

While raw view counts capture market share, the quality of audience interaction (engagement) behaves entirely differently.

The Deliverable

This final pillar of our report outlines actionable takeaways for optimizing digital platform performance:

  • Four Key Insights: The core behaviors uncovered by our data and dashboards.
  • Five Strategic Pillars: Concrete recommendations for brands, marketers, and creators to maximize growth.

Key Business Insights (1/2)

1. The "Viral Dilution" Effect

Engagement Decreases with Scale

  • The Inverse Relationship: As demonstrated by our scatter plot analysis, there is a clear inverse relationship between view volume and engagement rates.
  • The Phenomenon: Videos that achieve massive, viral reach tend to suffer from "diluted" audience interaction. Higher views reliably correspond to lower engagement rates.
  • Takeaway: Viral reach guarantees eyeballs, but it does not guarantee active interaction like likes or comments.

2. Engagement is Highly Concentrated

The 1% Rule

  • Severe Inequality: Our histogram distribution proves extreme performance inequality across the entire platform.
  • The Bottom 99%: The vast majority of videos fall into the absolute lowest engagement ranges (0% to 2.2%).
  • The Breakouts: Only a fractional percentage of videos manage to break out and achieve strong, concentrated audience engagement.

Key Business Insights (2/2)

3. Scale-Adjusted Leaders Exist

Neutralizing Big Channel Bias

  • A New Tier of Creators: When we neutralize the "big channel bias" using our engineered engagement metrics, new leaders emerge.
  • Engagement Quality: Certain channels consistently outperform the market average in pure engagement quality, regardless of their total subscriber or view counts.
  • Takeaway: Creator quality and community loyalty vary significantly across the ecosystem.

4. Geographic & Temporal Differences Matter

Engagement is not a monolith

  • Regional Variance: Our geospatial mapping and country-level boxplots reveal that engagement levels vary drastically by region.
  • Concentrated Pockets: Momentum is heavily concentrated in specific regional pockets.
  • Takeaway: This proves that cultural consumption habits directly dictate user interaction rates.

Strategic Recommendations (1/2)

I. Shift Focus Beyond Views

Brands and marketers must fundamentally stop optimizing solely for reach.

  • Re-align KPIs: Success metrics must be re-aligned to prioritize engagement quality.
  • Active Conversion: Strengthen internal Content Calls-to-Action (CTAs) to actively convert passive viewers into participating users.

II. Scale What Already Works

>5% Threshold Strategy

  • Action: Identify the specific, rare videos surpassing a strong engagement threshold (e.g., >5%).
  • Analyze: Evaluate their format, tone, pacing, and structure.
  • Goal: Reverse-engineer these anomalies and replicate those formats to turn accidental engagement success into a repeatable content strategy.

Strategic Recommendations (2/2)

III. Partner with High-Quality Channels

Shift influencer and partnership budgets away from pure "vanity metrics" like raw subscribers or views.

  • Data-Driven Collabs: Collaborate instead with creators who rank highest in our Scale-Adjusted Engagement Quality metric.
  • Benchmarking: Use these high-performing channels as standard benchmarks for your own internal content.

IV. Hyper-Localize Strategy

Standardized global content fails to maximize engagement across the board.

  • Invest in Momentum: Invest heavily in the high-momentum regions identified in the geographic analysis.
  • Cultural Adaptation: Content must be culturally adapted and localized to match specific regional audience behaviors.

V. Strategic Conclusion

Sustainable platform growth requires balancing massive reach with high-quality engagement, leveraging unique regional strengths, and scientifically replicating proven, high-performing content patterns.
